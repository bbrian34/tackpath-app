Frequently Asked
How do I delete a job?
Click the job card in the dispatcher to open the detail drawer, scroll to the bottom and click the red Delete Job button.
What is the OCA?
The Operations Coordinator Agent runs every 60 seconds. It watches all active jobs and drivers, detects problems like stalled deliveries or unassigned jobs, and creates work items for you to approve.
What is Ruby?
Ruby is the AI assistant built into the driver app. The purple Ruby button is always visible on the delivery screen. Drivers tap it to ask questions, report problems, or get help at any stop.
What is the difference between Sprint and Surge?
Sprint is a single pickup to a single dropoff -- one package, one stop, created via the Customer Portal. Surge is a multi-stop block route with multiple packages and stops, created via SmartSort.
How do I contact a driver?
Open the job drawer by clicking the job, then click Contact Driver. For a full message thread, go to Symphony+ and click the Comms tab -- select the driver on the left to open their channel.
What is the exception queue?
The exception queue is the right column in Symphony+ Dispatch view. It shows AI-detected problems -- stalled jobs, long-pending orders, SLA risks. Click Approve to action the recommendation or Reject to dismiss it.
How does a driver sign in?
Drivers open driver.html on their phone browser, enter their mobile number, tap Send Code, enter the verification code sent to their phone, and tap Verify and Sign In. No password or app download needed.
How do I upload a manifest in SmartSort?
Go to smartsort.html, drag and drop your CSV file onto the page or click to browse. Click Download CSV Template first if you need the correct column format. Then click Run SmartSort to build routes.
How does billing work?
Go to Symphony+ and click the Billing tab. Click Run Billing Agent -- Claude reviews all delivered jobs, verifies proof of delivery exists, and prepares invoice line items. You approve each one before it is sent.
What does a confidence score mean?
Every AI decision includes a confidence score. 90%+ means the agent is highly certain. 75-89% means it needs your approval before acting. Below 75% means the agent is uncertain and requires you to make the call.
What is the difference between Symphony+ and TackPath 1?
Symphony+ is the operations command center for dispatchers -- fleet status, comms, incidents, billing. TackPath 1 is the owner intelligence dashboard -- revenue, agent decisions, client health, work items needing approval.
What is proof of delivery (POD)?
POD is required at every stop before it can be marked complete. The driver collects either a signature (recipient signs on the phone screen) or a photo (picture of the package at the door). Without POD the stop cannot be confirmed.
What do I do when no driver accepts a job?
If a job stays pending for 15+ minutes the OCA will automatically flag it in the exception queue. You can also check driver availability in Symphony+ Fleet Map and rebroadcast the job to get it picked up faster.

Getting Started
What is TackPath?
TackPath is an AI-powered dispatch platform built for small courier companies. It handles order intake, driver dispatch, real-time tracking, proof of delivery, and invoicing -- with AI agents working in the background so your team can focus on the deliveries, not the admin.
DISP
01 — Dispatcher Portal
Create and manage jobs, broadcast to drivers, monitor every active delivery in real time, and communicate directly with your fleet from one screen.
DRV
02 — Driver App
Drivers receive job offers instantly on their phone, navigate to each stop, scan packages, collect proof of delivery, and communicate with dispatch.
CUST
03 — Customer Portal
Clients place delivery orders and track their packages in real time. Automatic status updates are sent at every stage -- no calls to your office required.
+
04 — Symphony+
The operations command center for dispatchers and managers. Fleet status, AI agent feed, incident management, driver communications, and billing in one view.
1
05 — TackPath 1
The owner intelligence dashboard. Revenue, client health scores, AI agent decisions, and work items needing approval. The full picture of your business.
SORT
06 — SmartSort
Upload a delivery manifest in CSV format. TackPath organizes the stops into optimized routes and broadcasts them directly to your available drivers.

Handling Exceptions
TackPath's AI automatically flags problems before they become failures. Here is where they appear and what to do.
01 — The Exception Queue
Right column in Dispatch view
The Exception Queue is the right column on the Dispatch screen in Symphony+. It shows every flag raised by the Operations Coordinator Agent -- stalled deliveries, jobs pending too long with no driver, SLA risks. Each item shows the problem, the job affected, and the AI confidence score.
Tap ✓ Approve to execute the recommendation, or ✕ Reject to dismiss it. The AI learns from your decisions over time.
Note: Items expire after 2 hours if not actioned. High priority items are flagged in orange, critical in red.

SmartSort: Uploading a Route Manifest
Have a list of deliveries to run? Upload your manifest CSV and SmartSort builds the routes automatically and broadcasts them to your drivers.
01 — Upload Your CSV
Go to SmartSort → upload your file
Navigate to smartsort.html. Click the upload area and select your CSV file -- or drag and drop it directly onto the page. Your file should include order ID, recipient name, delivery address, and package count at minimum. Not sure of the format? Click Download CSV Template to get the exact layout.
Tip: SmartSort reads barcodes directly from your existing manifests. Whatever barcode is on the package will be used for scanning during delivery.

Accepting a Job
When a route is broadcast, every available driver gets an offer. First to accept gets the job.
01 — The Job Offer
Accept or Decline
When a new job is broadcast, it appears on the driver's screen automatically with a sound alert. The offer shows the pickup address, dropoff address, number of stops, and estimated pay. The driver taps ✓ Accept to take the job or ✕ Decline to pass. If they decline, the offer goes to the next available driver.
Important: Once a driver accepts, the job is theirs. The route loads immediately and they can begin navigation to the first stop.

02 — At Stop → Scan → POD
Three taps per delivery
When the driver arrives at a stop they tap At Stop to confirm arrival. Then tap Scan Package to scan the barcode on the package using their phone camera. Once scanned, they tap Confirm & Next ✓ which opens the Proof of Delivery screen -- they collect either a signature or a photo, then submit. The stop is marked complete and they move to the next one.
Ruby AI: The purple Ruby button is always visible. If the driver has a question or problem at any stop, they can tap Ruby and speak to the AI assistant for help.

03 — Proof of Delivery
Signature or photo -- driver's choice
The POD screen gives the driver two options. Signature -- the recipient signs directly on the driver's phone screen. Photo -- the driver takes a photo of the delivered package at the door. Either option is accepted and stored automatically in TackPath. Once submitted, the stop is marked with a green checkmark in the route list.
Important: POD is required before a stop can be confirmed. A delivery cannot be marked complete without it.

Driver App
Ruby — AI Assistant
Ruby is TackPath's AI assistant built into the driver app. It is always one tap away, no matter where the driver is in the delivery flow.
💬
What Ruby can do
Drivers tap the purple Ruby button at any time to speak or type a question. Ruby can answer questions about the current delivery, help with navigation issues, contact dispatch, flag a problem at a stop, or provide guidance on handling exceptions. Ruby is context-aware -- it already knows which job the driver is on, which stop they are at, and what has been delivered so far.
Customer Portal
Placing an Order
Customers can place delivery orders directly without calling your office. The portal is accessible from any browser, no app download needed.
01 — Filling Out the Order Form
Pickup, dropoff, package details
The customer opens the Customer Portal and fills in the pickup address, delivery address, recipient name, package type, and any special notes. They select a priority level -- Standard, Priority, or Critical -- which affects dispatch speed. When ready, they tap Request Quote to see the price, then Confirm & Dispatch to place the order.
Note: Once an order is placed it immediately appears in the Dispatcher Portal and Symphony+ as a new pending job. Your team will see it within seconds.

AI Agents
TackPath runs AI agents in the background so your team can focus on deliveries. Here is what each agent does and when it runs.
AgentWhat it doesWhen it runsStatus
Operations Coordinator (OCA) Watches all active jobs and drivers. Detects stalled deliveries, unassigned jobs, SLA risks. Creates work items for the owner. Every 60 seconds LIVE
Communication Agent Sends automatic delivery updates to customers when job status changes. Logs every message sent. On every status change LIVE
Intake Agent Validates and classifies every new job. Flags missing addresses, unusual pricing, and incomplete orders before dispatch. Every 10 seconds LIVE
Billing Agent Reviews completed deliveries, verifies POD, and prepares invoice line items for owner approval. On demand READY
Dispatch Agent (SmartPath) Assigns optimal drivers based on location, workload, and real-time traffic. Requires Google Maps API. On job creation PENDING
⚠️
About AI Decisions
Every AI recommendation appears as a work item in TackPath 1 and Symphony+ before anything is executed. The AI suggests, you decide. Nothing is done automatically without your approval unless you have specifically enabled autonomous actions for that agent.
